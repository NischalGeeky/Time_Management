  1. Prepare a TO-DO List


    It's essential to plan out your daily tasks to ensure clarity and conciseness throughout your day. A To-Do List serves as a compilation of all the important tasks to be completed on a given day.Though it may seem like a small list, it provides wonderful clarity, enabling you to work according to your plans without wasting time contemplating your next task.

  2. Timetable

    Time Work
    6:00-7:00 Gym Workout
    7:00-8:00 Bath, Breakfast
    8:00-9:00 Review Yesterday's Work
    9:00-9:15 Break
    9:15-10:00 Solve Maths Problems
    10:00-10:05 Drinks Break
    10:05-11:05 DSA
    11:00-11:10 Go for A Walk
    11:10-12:00 Web Development
    12:10-1:00 Lunch

    A timetable organizes and allocates time effectively, providing a structured plan for daily or periodic activities. It enhances productivity, reduces procrastination, and helps prioritize tasks. By creating a visual roadmap, a timetable aids in efficient time management, ensuring tasks are completed in a systematic and timely manner.

    Key points to note while making a time table

    • Prioritize tasks
    • Realistic Planning
    • Include Breaks
    • Allow Flexibility
    • Balance your activities

  3. Detox Distractions

    Steps to Detox Distractions

    • Turn on Do Not Disturb Mode on your Gadget.
    • Set Time Limit for your Social Media.
    • Set an alarm for every 30 min.
    • Practice Meditation and Yoga.
    • Get Inspired from people who perform better in their work and learn their idealogies to minimize distractions.
    • Keep Journaling your screen time everyday to notice the improvement
    • Maintain a diary to mention the exact changes you felt after detoxing distractions

  4. Pomodoro Technique

    The Pomodoro Technique is a time-management method developed by Francesco Cirillo. It involves breaking work into 25-minute intervals (Pomodoros) separated by short breaks. After four Pomodoros, take a longer break. This helps maintain focus, productivity, and prevents burnout by incorporating regular breaks into the work routine.

  5. Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)

    The 80-20 Rule, or Pareto Principle, states that roughly 80% of effects come from 20% of causes. It implies that a minority of efforts yield the majority of results. In business, 80% of sales often come from 20% of customers. Identifying and focusing on the crucial 20% can optimize productivity and resource allocation, maximizing impact with minimal effort.

  6. Two-minute Rule

    The Two-Minute Rule, popularized by productivity expert David Allen, suggests that if a task takes less than two minutes to complete, do it immediately rather than postponing it. This simple strategy helps in efficiently handling quick tasks, preventing them from accumulating and becoming overwhelming, ultimately enhancing productivity. This technique promotes the reduction of procastination.

  7. Read Motivational Books

    Reading Motivational books reduces stress and ignites new energy within to achieve more. It helps us flush out the workaholic day and gives a remainder that we have a long way to go.

  8. Work-Life Balance

    Work-life balance is a state of equilibrium where a person can effectively manage their time, energy, and resources between their work and personal life.

    • Time Blocking

      Allocate specific time blocks for work, family, and personal activities. This helps maintain a structured routine and ensures dedicated time for each aspect of life.

    • Quality Over Quantity

      Focus on the quality of work rather than the quantity. Efficient, high-quality work allows for more time outside of work hours.
